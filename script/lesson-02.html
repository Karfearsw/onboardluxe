Cold Calling Mastery

New Hire · SDR · Agent 8 min read Prerequisite: Lesson 01

The 4-Step Call Flow

Cold calling is a skill — repeatable, improvable, and measurable. This structure gives you a framework to run every call consistently.

  1. Open — Who you are, reason for calling, and ask for permission to continue. This earns you the next 30 seconds.
  2. Qualify — Ask a few focused questions to determine if this person is a fit. Don't pitch before you qualify.
  3. Value — One clear reason the next step is worth their time. Make it specific to what they told you.
  4. Next Step — Book the appointment or agree on a specific follow-up date. This is the only goal of the call.

Opener Scripts

Replace the bracketed parts with the lead's actual details. Practice these until they feel natural — you should never sound like you're reading.

Opener A — Permission-Based
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Ocean Luxe. I'm calling because [reason tied to lead source]. Do you have 30 seconds so I can tell you why I'm reaching out?"
Opener B — Curiosity + Clarity
"Hi [Name] — [Your Name], Ocean Luxe. Quick question: are you still interested in [property goal / situation], or did plans change?"
Pro Tip

Opener A is better for cold lists where you have a clear reason (e.g., "we saw your property was listed"). Opener B works well for re-engagement when you already have their info and want to cut to the chase.

Qualifying Questions

These three questions give you the information you need to determine if a follow-up or appointment makes sense. Ask them in a conversational tone — not as an interrogation.

  • "What's the timeline you're working with?" — This reveals urgency and whether they're a near-term or long-term opportunity.
  • "What does a good outcome look like for you?" — This surfaces their actual goal, not just their stated situation.
  • "Any deal-breakers I should know up front?" — This catches red flags early and shows you respect their time.

Next-Step Language

A next step is only a next step if it has a specific date, time, and action. Vague next steps get forgotten.

Booking a call
"The best next step is a quick 10–15 minute call with [role]. Would [Day/Time] or [Day/Time] work for you?"
If not ready
"No problem — what date should I follow up so this doesn't fall through the cracks?"

Outcome Logging

Every call must be logged — even if no one picks up. This protects the lead pipeline and helps the team pick up where you left off.

  • Date and time of the attempt
  • Outcome: No Answer · Callback Requested · Qualified · Not a Fit · Do Not Contact
  • Key notes from the conversation (goal, timeline, objections)
  • Agreed next step with specific date and time

Practice Script (5 minutes)

Write your opener (A or B)
Your 3 qualifying questions
Next-step sentence (for booking + for follow-up)
