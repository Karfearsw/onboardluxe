Cold Calling Mastery

New Hire · SDR · Agent 8 min read Prerequisite: Lesson 01

The 4-Step Call Flow

Cold calling is a skill — repeatable, improvable, and measurable. This structure gives you a framework to run every call consistently.

  1. Open — Who you are, reason for calling, and ask for permission to continue. This earns you the next 30 seconds.
  2. Qualify — Ask a few focused questions to determine if this person is a fit. Don't pitch before you qualify.
  3. Value — One clear reason the next step is worth their time. Make it specific to what they told you.
  4. Next Step — Book the appointment or agree on a specific follow-up date. This is the only goal of the call.

Core Seller Script (Team Standard)

This is the baseline seller opener we use. Replace brackets with real details, sound casual, and pause after the question.

Confirm identity
You: “Hey, is this [Homeowner’s Name]?”
Baseline opener
You: “Hey [Name], this is [Your Name]. I know this call is out of the blue so I’ll be quick — I was calling about the property on [Address]. I was wondering if you’d consider selling it if the price made sense?”
Rule

Ask the question, then pause. Silence makes people talk.

Branch Playbooks

Your job is to find motivation and lock a real next step (appointment or follow-up date/time). Use these branches so you don’t improvise under pressure.

If they say “Maybe / Depends”
You: “Gotcha, that makes sense. If you don’t mind me asking, have you thought about selling soon or were you just curious what you could get for it?”
  • “Is the property currently rented or vacant?”
  • “What kind of condition is it in right now?”
  • “Do you have a price in mind you’d want for it?”
If they say “Yes, I’d sell”
You: “Nice, that’s good to know. Just so I don’t waste your time — if we could agree on a fair price, would you be open to selling in the next 30–60 days?”
  • “Are you the sole owner of the property or is there anyone else involved?”
  • “Does the house need any repairs?”
  • “What would you ideally want to walk away with?”
  • “What does a good closing timeline look like for you?” If they’re unsure: suggest 15–35 days and explain you handle closing costs.
Non-negotiable

If they are interested, get their email.

If they say “No”
You: “No worries at all. I figured it wouldn’t hurt to ask. If you ever did decide to sell down the line, would it be okay if I reached back out?”

Sometimes they say “maybe later” or “call me in a year.” That’s future pipeline — log it.

If they ask “How did you get my number?”
You: “I work with a small group that buys properties in the area, so we were looking at homes nearby and your property came up.”

Qualify + Next Step (Real Estate Version)

Don’t pitch before you qualify. Your job is to figure out motivation and lock an appointment or a specific follow-up.

  • Timeline first: “Have you thought about selling soon, or were you just curious?”
  • Occupancy: rented vs vacant
  • Condition: repairs needed
  • Price expectation: “What would you want to walk away with?”
  • Decision makers: “Are you the sole owner or is anyone else involved?” (use when it’s getting real)
Booking an appointment
“The best next step is a quick 10–15 minute call so we can go over the details and talk numbers. Would [Day/Time] or [Day/Time] work for you?”
If you need to run numbers first (hot lead)
Option 1: “Can I put you on a brief hold while I run quick numbers?”
Option 2: “Let me run numbers on my end and I’ll call you back at [Day/Time]. Does that work?”
If not ready
“No problem — what date should I follow up so this doesn’t fall through the cracks?”

Required Data Capture + Outcome Logging

Every attempt gets logged — even no-answer calls. If the lead is interested, capture the details the team needs to move the deal forward.

  • Date and time of the attempt
  • Outcome: No Answer · Callback Requested · Qualified · Not a Fit · Do Not Contact
  • Timeline, occupancy, condition, price expectation, decision makers
  • Email address (required for interested leads)
  • Agreed next step with specific date and time

Key Cold Calling Tips (Most Important)

  • Sound casual. Talk like you’re calling a neighbor.
  • Pause after the question. Silence makes people talk.
  • Smile while talking. It changes your voice.
  • Your only goal is to find motivation. Examples: tired landlord, inherited property, needs repairs, behind on payments, moving.
Tone example
Bad: “Hi I’m calling to see if you’re interested in selling your property.”
Good: “I know this call is random but I figured I’d ask…”
