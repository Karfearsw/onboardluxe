Handling Objections

New Hire · SDR · Agent 6 min read Prerequisite: Lesson 02

The ACR Framework

Objections are usually signals — timing, uncertainty, or lack of clarity. Your job is to stay calm, stay helpful, and still move toward a clear next step. The ACR framework gives you a repeatable way to do that on any objection.

Acknowledge → Clarify → Respond
One step at a time. Never skip Acknowledge — it's what keeps rapport alive.
  1. Acknowledge — Show you heard them. No arguing, no dismissing. A one-liner that proves you're listening, not just waiting for your turn to talk.
  2. Clarify — Ask one focused question to understand the real issue. The surface objection and the real objection are often different.
  3. Respond — Address the issue briefly, then pivot to a next step. Keep it short — don't over-explain.

Common Objections — ACR in Practice

"Not interested."
Acknowledge: "Totally fair."
Clarify: "Before I let you go — are you not interested in [goal], or is it just bad timing?"
Respond: If timing → "When would it make sense to follow up?" / If not a fit → "Understood — should I close this out so you don't hear from us again?"
"Just send me info."
Acknowledge: "Happy to."
Clarify: "What should the info focus on — timeline, pricing, or options?"
Respond: "I'll send that today. To make it relevant, can we do a quick 5-minute call first — today at [time] or tomorrow at [time]?"
"I'm too busy."
Acknowledge: "I get it."
Clarify: "Is later today better, or would tomorrow be easier?"
Respond: "Great — let's do [date/time]. I'll call you then."
Notice

Every Respond step ends with a next step. That's the pattern: Acknowledge → Clarify → Respond → next step. Every time.

When to Close Out a Call

Not every lead is worth pursuing indefinitely. Close out (per Ocean Luxe policy) when:

  • They explicitly opt out or request do-not-contact
  • It's clearly not a fit based on Ocean Luxe's criteria
  • They become abusive or unsafe to engage
Important

A clean close-out is not a failure — it's professionalism. Protect your time and the lead list by logging it correctly and moving on.
