Deal Analysis & ARV

New Hire · SDR · Agent 7 min read Prerequisite: Lesson 02

What ARV Means

Deal analysis is about consistency. You don't need perfection — you need a repeatable way to decide: advance, follow up, or escalate.

ARV = After Repair Value
The estimated market value of the property after all required repairs and updates are completed — not the current as-is value.

ARV is the anchor for every number that follows. Get this wrong and every downstream calculation is off.

Inputs needed to estimate ARV

  • Property address + basic specs (beds, baths, square footage)
  • Condition notes — what needs repair and how extensive
  • Comparable sales ("comps") — similar size, area, recently sold
  • Estimated repair budget — a high-level range is fine for screening

Screening Methods

Ocean Luxe uses one of these two methods for deal screening. Until a standard is set internally, know both.

Option A — 70% Rule (Quick Screen)
MAO = ARV × 0.70 − Repairs
MAO = Max Allowable Offer · Quick, widely used, conservative
Option B — Costs + Profit (Explicit)
MAO = ARV − Repairs − Holding Costs − Selling Costs − Target Profit
More transparent — shows exactly where every dollar goes

Escalation Risk Factors

A screening number doesn't mean the deal is ready to move. Escalate when you see any of these:

  • Unclear or unreliable comps — unusual property, no recent sales nearby, or drastically varying prices in the area
  • Unknown or high-variance repairs — foundation issues, full roof replacement, major system failures (HVAC, electrical, plumbing)
  • Title, lien, or occupancy complications — anything flagged by policy
  • Seller expectations far above screening range — if their number is 30%+ above your MAO, flag it before proceeding
Rule

When in doubt, escalate. It's always better to loop in a senior team member than to advance a deal with hidden risk.
